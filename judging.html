Judging and Running

Judging and running skills everyone should have because helping out helps keep competitions on schedule. This page should help you learn the basics.

Judging

Hint: Here's a cheat sheet for judging

Judging is not too difficult - you don't even have to be a cuber to do it well. While you don't have to know every single regulation, it is easier the more you know. However, since we know you can't memorize everything, we've prepared some cheat sheets with a list of penalties so you can refer to it when needed. If you're still not sure, just ask an experienced judge or the delegate - it's always better to be cautious!

Getting a Cube

To start, sit down at your judging station. You should be sitting on one of the short sides of the table. If the competition is using a runner system (which it usually is), just raise your hand and someone will bring you a cube cover with a cube and scoresheet. Otherwise, just walk up to the scrambling table and take one. Then, call out the competitor's name as it's written on the scorecard and wait for them to come over. If they don't come within a reasonable amount of time, get a different cube and let the delegate know.

Stopwatches

Although the delegate/organizer will provide stopwatches to time inspection, they usually aren't as easy to use as you might like. If you have a smartphone or tablet, the best option is to direct your browser to cubing.net/inspection. Now, just tap to start and tap to stop - it will flash at 8 and 12, which we'll talk about later. This timer resets automatically when started, so don't worry about that.

Starting the Solve

Take out the scorecard and put it next to you. Put the cube cover on top of the cube (so the cube is fully covered) and put the entire setup on the Stackmat. If this is the first round of the first event and you don't know the competitor, ask if they've competed before and if not, let them practice starting and stopping the Stackmat. Before they start the solve, there are a few things you should check for - the competitor can't have electronic headphones or earbuds, they can't have a personal light, and they can't have any kind of screen or mirror facing them. This means that they can take a video, as long as they're not able to see the video. They also can't be talking to anyone during the solve. Ask the competitor if they are ready, and when they say yes, simultaneously lift the cube cover and tap your device to start the timer.

Ending the Solve

If the competitor started after more than 15 seconds of inspection, they get a penalty: a +2 if it's under 17 seconds or a DNF otherwise. Next, make sure the cube is solved. If one side is over 45 degrees off, there is a +2 penalty. If more than one side is more than 45 degrees off, it's a DNF. On the competitor's scorecard, write down the time, any penalties, and the total time including penalties. If it's a DNF, write DNF.

Running

Running is also very important, and is easy to do. If you want to help out but aren't comfortable judging (or there are no open stations), this is a great option. Just go up to the scrambling table, take a cube cover with a cube in it, and give it to a judge who is raising their hand. Take any solved cubes in cube covers and bring those up to the scrambling table. At the scrambling table, put the scorecard face-up, place the cube on top, and put the cube cover to the side, and get more cubes to bring to judges.
